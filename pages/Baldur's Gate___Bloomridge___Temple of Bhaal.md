- In the bowels of Baldur's Gate lies its darkest secret—the Temple of Bhaal, hidden within the labyrinthine expanse of the Undercity. A place of eerie reverence, its halls echo with whispers of power, vengeance, and a thirst for blood. Followers of Bhaal, the God of Murder, skulk within its shadowy alcoves, carrying out gruesome rites that defy the imagination.
- Veiled in perpetual darkness, the temple's architecture is a grim monument to its malevolent deity: blood-stained altars, walls adorned with symbols of daggers and skulls, and the ever-present stench of fresh murder.
