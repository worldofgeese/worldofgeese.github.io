- Before the corruption brought by the malevolent Malar, [[Selûne]] was considered the principal deity for lycanthropes, particularly good-aligned ones.
- ### Victims of Ancient Crime
- When [[Malar]], the god of the hunt and wild beasts, began his corruption campaign against the lycanthrope clans, he tainted their lineage, leading some to lose control and commit monstrous acts. This event marked a historical point of suffering, as it altered the relationship between Selûne and her once-devoted followers. This is seen as an ancient crime against not just the lycanthropes but also against Selûne herself.
- ### Selune's Compassion and Guidance
- Selûne is not a vengeful goddess; she weeps for the victims of Malar's corruption. Her compassion leads her to offer guidance and peace to lycanthropes who open their hearts to her.
